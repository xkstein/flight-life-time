If there is one commercial plane crash every 20 million flights, and you've got more years to live in general, we can find your average lifespan if you take this flight. \text{your average lifespan after taking this flight} = \frac{(20 \text{ million} - 1) * x + 0}{20 \text{ million}} 20 million - 1 of you keep living for x longer while 1 of you dies on the plane, living 0 more time.

By subtracting this from your predicted lifespan, we can find the average amount of time it'll cost you to take this flight. \begin{align*}\text{Average time lost by dying in a flight} &= x - \frac{20 \text{ million}-1}{20 \text{ million}}x \newline&= \frac{1}{20 \text{ million}}x\end{align*}
